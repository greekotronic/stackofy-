Banned, Unbanned, and Still 64% Fake

Let's be honest about why you're here. Either you Googled "NMN supplements" and landed in a swamp of contradicting articles and shady Amazon listings - or you already bought a bottle, took it for two months, felt absolutely nothing, and are now wondering if you paid sixty dollars for expensive chalk. Both situations are common. Neither is your fault, and as you're about to see, the second one might literally be true.

Here's what most coverage of NMN leaves out entirely: this molecule spent three straight years in genuine legal limbo in the United States, banned outright as a dietary supplement by the FDA in late 2022, only to be un-banned in 2025 after an industry lawsuit forced the agency's hand. Through all of that legal whiplash, the underlying quality problem on store shelves never went away - independent testing kept finding the same thing it found before the ban: a huge share of products on the market simply don't contain meaningful amounts of the ingredient on the label.

What NMN Actually Does

NMN is a precursor - its job is to convert into NAD+ inside your cells. NAD+ is a coenzyme essential to energy production, DNA repair, and keeping cellular machinery functioning. Your NAD+ levels decline measurably with age: by 40, you've got roughly half of what you had at 20, and less again by 60. That decline tracks with things people notice directly - slower recovery, lower energy, brain fog, reduced resilience.

What NMN is not: a miracle cure, a substitute for sleep and exercise, or something you'll feel dramatically by day three. What a quality product can realistically do over weeks to months: support cellular energy pathways, measurably raise NAD+ levels (confirmable via blood test), and potentially slow some age-associated cellular decline - the mechanistic case is genuinely solid, even where the human outcome data is still maturing.

The Three-Year Regulatory Rollercoaster

This part of the NMN story gets almost no coverage, and it matters for understanding why quality control in this category has been so inconsistent.

NMN's Legal Status: A Three-Year Whiplash
May 2022
FDA acknowledges New Dietary Ingredient Notifications (NDINs) from SyncoZymes and Inner Mongolia Kingdomway, the standard regulatory green light for selling NMN as a supplement ingredient.
Nov 2022
★ FDA reverses itself, ruling that NMN is excluded from the legal definition of a dietary supplement under the "drug preclusion clause" - because a pharmaceutical company had already studied NMN as an investigational drug (MIB-626) before it was lawfully marketed as a supplement. Retailers including Amazon began pulling NMN listings.
2023-24
The Natural Products Association files a citizen petition, then a federal lawsuit, arguing NMN had clearly been marketed as a supplement before the drug investigation began. A court-ordered stay pauses enforcement while the FDA reconsiders.
Sept 2025
★ FDA completely reverses its 2022 position, confirming NMN "is not excluded from the definition of a dietary supplement" after all. By December 2025, the agency sends formal letters reinstating the original NDI notifications.
★ The entire three-year saga was a legal and regulatory dispute, never a safety finding - the FDA at no point declared NMN unsafe. But the back-and-forth illustrates how legally fragile a popular supplement category can be, and explains why some manufacturers paused production or reformulated during the ban, which may have contributed to ongoing inconsistency in what's actually on shelves today.
The Quality Problem Predates and Outlasts the Ban

Independent lab tests examining top-selling NMN supplements on Amazon found that 64% contained NMN levels below the detectable limit - effectively zero active ingredient. This testing was conducted around the same period as the regulatory chaos, and nothing about the FDA's 2025 reversal addresses product quality at all; it only resolved whether NMN can legally be sold. A supplement being legal and a supplement actually containing what it claims are two completely separate questions, and this category has a documented history of failing the second one at a striking rate.

The Human Trial Evidence - What's Actually Confirmed

Strong Evidence

Raises Blood NAD+ Levels

A January 2026 four-arm RCT (n=65) found NMN and NR doubled circulating NAD+ over 14 days, while plain nicotinamide (NAM) showed no significant increase - useful, direct evidence for which precursor name on a label actually moves the needle.

Moderate Evidence

Insulin Sensitivity (Prediabetic Women)

A controlled trial found NMN supplementation improved muscle insulin sensitivity in prediabetic postmenopausal women - one of the more frequently cited human metabolic outcomes, though in a specific population.

Moderate Evidence

Liposomal Form Outperforms Standard

A February 2025 trial in men over 40 found liposomal NMN raised NAD+ significantly more than non-liposomal NMN at the same 350mg dose - a meaningful formulation detail most product labels don't address.

Established Safety

Well-Tolerated at Studied Doses

A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled safety trial found 1,250mg/day oral β-NMN for 4 weeks was well-tolerated in healthy adults aged 20-65, supporting safety at doses considerably above typical consumer use.

NMN vs. NR - They're Not Identical, But They're Comparable

The January 2026 head-to-head trial is genuinely useful because it directly compared three NAD+ precursor approaches in the same people under the same conditions, rather than relying on cross-study comparisons. NMN and NR performed comparably at raising blood NAD+, with no statistically significant difference between them - despite years of marketing from each side claiming superiority. The one notable side finding: NAM (plain nicotinamide, the cheapest and most common vitamin B3 form) increased homocysteine without meaningfully raising NAD+, reinforcing why TMG supplementation alongside NMN/NR matters more than alongside NAM.

The 90-Second Quality Check

Once you know what to look for, filtering out the garbage takes about 90 seconds per product.

Before You Buy Any NMN Supplement

✓ Certificate of Analysis available? From an ISO 17025 accredited independent lab - not the company's own internal testing. If you can't find it within two clicks, walk away.
✓ Purity 98%+ for beta-NMN specifically? "High purity NMN" without a percentage means nothing. You need a specific number confirmed by the COA - and the COA and label should match.
✓ GMP certified facility? Look for "cGMP certified" or "FDA-registered facility." No GMP almost guarantees a questionable product.
✗ Wall of fillers or proprietary blends? Quality NMN needs nothing else. A long ingredient list full of additives means you're paying for padding.
✓ Price $40-80 per month at 500mg/day? Quality NMN costs money to produce properly. Significantly below that, someone cut corners.
✓ Reasonably current NDI/regulatory standing? Given the 2022-2025 legal back-and-forth, a brand that can point to a reinstated or original NDI notification has at least cleared a real regulatory bar, not just a marketing claim.

How to Dose It Correctly

Current human research points to a sweet spot of 250mg to 500mg per day for most people. Start at 250mg for two to four weeks, let your body adjust, then move to 500mg if you want to push further. Take it in the morning with breakfast and a fat source - NAD+ is connected to circadian rhythm, and taking it at night can interfere with sleep in some people.

Give it at least 60 days with a quality product before judging it. Anyone quitting after two weeks because they "didn't feel anything" is applying a standard the molecule was never going to meet that quickly.

The Stack Partner Nobody Talks About: TMG

When your body converts NMN to NAD+, it produces homocysteine as a byproduct. Elevated homocysteine is associated with cardiovascular issues and builds up silently. TMG (trimethylglycine, also called betaine) neutralizes that homocysteine through methylation - cleaning up the metabolic byproduct that NAD+ production leaves behind.

Dose: 500-1000mg daily alongside your NMN. Inexpensive, widely available, and almost always overlooked. This stops being optional once you're taking NMN seriously and consistently.

How to Measure Whether It's Working

You can feel great with terrible biomarkers, or feel nothing with excellent ones. If you're serious about this, measure rather than guess.

  • NAD+ blood test - companies like Jinfiniti offer at-home testing. Baseline before you start, retest at 90 days. Around $100, worth it for actual confirmation.
  • Homocysteine - confirms your TMG is doing its job. Ideal below 9 µmol/L.
  • The 2pm test - the mid-afternoon energy crash is one of the earliest subjective signs of changing NAD+ status. As levels improve, this dip often softens before other changes become noticeable.
