What NMN Actually Does

NMN is a precursor. Its whole job is to convert into something called NAD+ inside your cells. NAD+ is the fuel your cells run on - involved in energy production, DNA repair, and keeping your cellular machinery from falling apart.

The problem? Your NAD+ levels drop off a cliff as you age. By the time you hit 40, you've got roughly half the NAD+ you had at 20. That decline shows up in ways you've probably noticed - slower recovery, lower energy, brain fog, the feeling that your body just doesn't bounce back the way it used to.

That's where NMN comes in. You take NMN, your body converts it to NAD+, NAD+ levels go back up, and your cells start functioning more like they did a decade ago. Simple in theory. Messy in practice - which is exactly why we're here.

How to Spot Garbage Before You Buy It

Once you know what to look for, filtering out the garbage takes about 90 seconds per product.

The 90-Second Quality Check

✓ Certificate of Analysis available? A COA from an ISO 17025 accredited independent lab confirms what's actually in the bottle. If you can't find it within two clicks - red flag.
✓ Purity 98%+ for beta-NMN specifically? "High purity NMN" without a percentage means nothing. You need a specific number confirmed by the COA.
✓ GMP certified facility? No GMP certification almost guarantees a questionable product.
✗ Wall of fillers or proprietary blends? Quality NMN needs nothing else. Fillers mean you're paying for padding.
✓ Price $40-80 per month at 500mg/day? Significantly below that - someone cut corners. Suspiciously cheap almost always means fake or underdosed.

The Dosing Formula

Current human research points to a sweet spot of 250mg to 500mg per day for most people. Start at 250mg for the first two to four weeks, let your body adjust, then move to 500mg if you want to push further.

Take NMN in the morning. NAD+ is deeply connected to your circadian rhythm - taking it at night can potentially disrupt sleep. Morning, with breakfast and a fat source. Not complicated but consistently ignored.

The Stack That Makes NMN Work Harder

NMN alone is good. NMN with the right additions is significantly better. The key partner most people overlook is TMG (Trimethylglycine).

When your body converts NMN to NAD+, it produces homocysteine as a byproduct. Elevated homocysteine is associated with cardiovascular issues. TMG neutralizes that homocysteine. Dose: 500-1000mg daily alongside your NMN. Inexpensive, widely available, and almost always overlooked. Don't skip it.
